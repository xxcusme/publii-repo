10 Things to Do in Bali for an Unforgettable Island Experience

Bali offers a variety of experiences that fit many interests, from nature lovers to culture seekers. You can visit stunning beaches, explore ancient temples, and enjoy unique local traditions all in one trip. Whether you want to relax or be active, Bali has options that suit your pace and style.

This list of 10 things to do in Bali will give you a clear idea of how to make the most of your visit. You will find activities like hiking to see sunrises, visiting rice terraces, and experiencing vibrant local markets. These highlights cover the best of Bali’s natural beauty and cultural richness.

Explore Bali’s Iconic Temples

Bali is home to temples that blend stunning settings with deep spiritual meaning. When you visit, you’ll see temples perched on cliffs, sitting by the sea, and rising above lush valleys. These places often host important ceremonies and offer insight into Bali’s unique culture and religion.

Visit Uluwatu Temple

Uluwatu Temple stands on a high cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean. You’ll find dramatic views of the water and sunset here. The temple is famous for its traditional Kecak dance performances held every evening. This dance tells stories from ancient Hindu epics.

When you visit, dress respectfully, as this is an active place of worship. Watch out for the monkeys around the temple; they can be bold but are part of the experience. Uluwatu offers a mix of natural beauty, culture, and spirituality that is very special.

Experience Tanah Lot at Sunset

Tanah Lot is one of Bali’s most popular temples because of its unique location on a rocky outcrop in the sea. At low tide, you can walk close to the temple along the beach.

Sunsets here are stunning and attract many visitors. The temple is sacred to Balinese people who come to pray and hold ceremonies by the sea. You can enjoy local markets nearby where you’ll find souvenirs and snacks.

If you want the best photos, arrive about an hour before sunset. The changing colors and waves hitting the rocks create a perfect backdrop.

Discover Besakih Temple

Besakih is Bali’s largest and holiest temple, often called the “Mother Temple.” It sits on the slopes of Mount Agung, Bali’s highest volcano. This temple complex includes 23 separate temples with intricate stone carvings and beautiful architecture.

Visiting Besakih gives you a deep look at Balinese Hindu culture. It is still used for important ceremonies and celebrations. Because of its size, you may want to join a guided tour to learn about the history and meaning of different parts.

Wear modest clothing and be ready to climb some stairs to see all the main areas of the complex. The views of the mountain and surrounding landscape are impressive.

Enjoy Bali’s Beaches

Bali offers a variety of beaches that fit different moods and activities. You can find spots to relax with a drink, catch waves, or swim in clear water surrounded by coral. Each beach has its own unique vibe and features that make it worth visiting.

Relax at Seminyak Beach

Seminyak Beach is perfect if you want to enjoy calm and stylish surroundings. The beach stretches wide, giving you space to lie on the soft sand or take a peaceful walk. Many beachfront cafes and bars line the shore, serving cold drinks and snacks.

The sunset here is a popular attraction, so arriving in the late afternoon lets you watch the sky change while you relax. Though busier than some other beaches, it still offers a laid-back atmosphere with good facilities, such as sunbeds and umbrellas for rent.

Surf at Kuta Beach

Kuta Beach is known for its consistent, easy waves, making it a top choice if you want to surf or take lessons. The water is shallow near the shore, so beginners can practice without much worry. You’ll also find plenty of surf schools and rental shops along the beach.

The area around Kuta is lively, with many shops and restaurants. You can surf in the morning, then enjoy a casual meal or shop for beachwear nearby. This beach is also a hub for socializing and meeting other travelers interested in water sports.

Swim at Blue Lagoon Beach

Blue Lagoon Beach offers clear water and calm conditions that make swimming safe and enjoyable. The bay is surrounded by rocks, which shelter the water from strong currents. This makes it perfect for families or anyone wanting a peaceful swim.

There’s coral to explore if you bring snorkeling gear, adding interest beneath the surface. The beach is smaller and less crowded than others, helping you avoid noise and crowds. You can also enjoy fresh seafood from local vendors close to the beach entrance.

Immerse in Balinese Culture

When you visit Bali, you can experience its culture through dance and food. Both offer a deep look into local traditions and daily life. These activities connect you with the island’s heritage and give you memories beyond the typical sights.

Watch Traditional Dance Performances

Balinese dance shows are a must-see. You’ll find performances at temples, cultural centers, and resorts. These dances tell stories from ancient myths and local history using detailed hand gestures, facial expressions, and costumes.

One famous dance is the Barong, which represents the fight between good and evil. Another popular show is the Kecak, performed by a large group chanting rhythmically. These performances often happen in the evening, creating a special atmosphere with traditional music.

Watching a dance allows you to appreciate Bali’s artistic skill and spiritual beliefs. It’s best to arrive early and choose shows known for authentic presentations. Photos are usually allowed, but confirm before the show starts.

Participate in a Balinese Cooking Class

Taking a cooking class lets you explore Balinese culture through food. You’ll visit local markets to buy fresh ingredients like spices, vegetables, and herbs. The class teacher shows you how to prepare traditional dishes step by step.

Typical meals you’ll learn to cook include Nasi Goreng (fried rice) and Satay (grilled meat skewers). Classes often explain the meaning behind certain foods and their role in local ceremonies. This hands-on experience gives you practical skills and a better understanding of daily life.

Most cooking schools provide all tools and ingredients. The classes usually last 3–4 hours and end with a shared meal where you get to taste your creation. This is an engaging way to connect with Balinese culture beyond sightseeing.

Discover Bali’s Natural Wonders

Bali offers some of the most beautiful natural sites you can explore. These places show a mix of stunning landscapes and peaceful settings. You can experience active volcanoes, lush green terraces, and powerful waterfalls all in one trip.

Hike Mount Batur at Sunrise

Mount Batur is an active volcano popular for sunrise hikes. You start climbing very early, usually around 3 or 4 a.m., to reach the summit by dawn. The hike takes about 2 hours and is moderately challenging.

At the top, you will see the sun rise over a lake and other mountains. The sky’s colors are truly worth the early wake-up. Bring warm clothes because it gets cold near the peak, and wear sturdy shoes since the path is rocky.

Explore Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Tegallalang Rice Terraces are famous for their beautiful, layered fields. These terraces show traditional Balinese farming techniques. Walking here gives you a chance to enjoy the greenery and see local farmers at work.

The area has paths between the terraces, perfect for taking photos or relaxing. You can also visit nearby cafes with views overlooking the fields. The site is easily accessible from Ubud, Bali’s cultural center.

Visit Sekumpul Waterfall

Sekumpul Waterfall is one of Bali’s tallest and most impressive waterfalls. It’s located in the northern part of the island. To reach the falls, you have to hike through forest paths and cross some small streams.

You’ll see several waterfalls in the area, not just one, making it great for nature lovers. The water is cool and fresh if you want to swim. Remember to wear comfortable shoes since the terrain can be slippery.

Experience Ubud’s Arts and Markets

You will find rich culture and nature woven together in Ubud. It is a place where playful monkeys roam and colorful crafts fill the air with creativity. The area offers hands-on experiences to connect with Bali’s artistic side and natural surroundings.

Tour Ubud Monkey Forest

When you visit Ubud Monkey Forest, expect to see a lush, green sanctuary filled with long-tailed macaques. This forest is more than just a home for monkeys; it also contains ancient temples hidden among towering trees.

As you walk through the forest, you’ll notice the monkeys interacting with each other and sometimes with visitors. Stay alert and follow local guidelines, such as not feeding the monkeys or carrying shiny objects. This helps keep both you and the animals safe.

The forest trails are easy to explore on foot. Take your time to enjoy the peaceful sounds of nature while spotting stone statues and temple carvings that reveal Bali’s spiritual heritage.

Shop at Ubud Art Market

Ubud Art Market is where you can buy traditional Balinese goods directly from local artists. You’ll find everything from hand-woven baskets and silk scarves to wooden masks and silver jewelry.

Prices can vary, so be ready to bargain politely. It’s part of the shopping experience and can get you a fair deal. Take time to look closely at the craftsmanship. Many items here reflect Balinese culture and stories.

The market is busiest in the morning but also offers a lively atmosphere late into the day. It’s a good spot to find souvenirs or gifts while supporting local artisans.

Relax with Balinese Wellness

Bali offers a deep sense of calm through its traditional wellness practices. You can restore your body with healing massages or find peace through yoga surrounded by nature’s beauty. Both offer ways to escape daily stress and feel refreshed.

Book a Balinese Spa Treatment

Balinese spa treatments focus on healing with natural ingredients like herbs, flowers, and oils. You can choose from massages that ease muscle tension, boost circulation, and improve relaxation. Many spas blend ancient techniques with modern comfort.

Popular treatments include the traditional Balinese massage, which uses long strokes and gentle pressure, and flower petal baths for added softness and aroma. Spas often offer packages that combine massages with body scrubs and facial treatments to give you a full wellness experience.

Booking in advance is recommended, especially at well-known spas near Ubud or beachfront resorts. These places provide serene environments that enhance your sense of calm. Your session usually lasts between 60 and 120 minutes, giving you plenty of time to unwind.

Try Yoga in the Jungle

Practicing yoga in Bali’s jungle offers a unique way to connect with nature while improving your mind and body. Many retreats set studios outdoors or in open-air pavilions surrounded by tropical greenery.

Yoga styles range from gentle flow to more vigorous power sessions, suited for all levels. Instructors often include meditation and breathing exercises to deepen relaxation and mental clarity.

Choosing a jungle yoga class lets you escape city noise. The sounds of birds and rustling leaves create a peaceful backdrop. Classes often last about 60 to 90 minutes and can be booked as single sessions or part of longer wellness programs.

This natural setting supports stress relief and helps you recharge physically and mentally.

Adventure Activities in Bali

Bali offers thrilling activities that let you explore the island in exciting ways. You can experience fast-moving rivers or navigate rugged terrain, both providing unique views and adrenaline.

Go White Water Rafting

White water rafting on Bali’s rivers gives you a mix of excitement and nature. You’ll paddle through strong rapids while surrounded by lush jungle and rice terraces. The Ayung River near Ubud is the most popular spot, with rapids suitable for beginners and intermediate rafters.

Tours usually include safety gear, experienced guides, and transportation. Along the way, you might see waterfalls and local wildlife. Rafting trips last about two to three hours, making it a good half-day adventure. Remember to wear quick-dry clothes and bring a change of clothes. This activity is ideal if you want action with natural beauty.

Take an ATV Tour

An ATV tour is a great way to explore Bali’s countryside off the beaten path. You’ll ride all-terrain vehicles over dirt tracks, hills, and sometimes muddy sections. These tours often include stops at villages, rice fields, and scenic viewpoints.

You don’t need prior experience, as guides provide instructions and safety gear like helmets. Tours last from one to two hours, offering a fun, hands-on way to see rural Bali. Wear sturdy shoes and comfortable clothes you don’t mind getting dirty. This is perfect if you want adventure combined with getting close to Bali’s landscapes and culture.

Explore Bali’s Underwater World

Bali offers many unique underwater spots where you can see colorful fish, coral reefs, and fascinating sea life. Whether you want to snorkel in calm waters or dive near a historic shipwreck, the options are diverse and memorable.

Snorkel in Nusa Lembongan

Nusa Lembongan is a small island southeast of Bali, known for its clear waters and rich marine life. When you snorkel here, you’ll float above vibrant coral reefs filled with tropical fish and sometimes spot sea turtles. The calm waters make it easy for beginners and families to enjoy.

Areas like the Blue Lagoon and Mangrove Point are popular snorkeling spots. You can expect to see bright corals and a wide variety of marine creatures close to the surface. Many tours include masks, fins, and guidance, so you don’t need prior experience.

Dive at Tulamben Shipwreck

Tulamben is famous for the USAT Liberty shipwreck lying just a short swim from the shore. This site is one of Bali’s top diving spots. The wreck is covered in coral and hosts many marine species, making it an underwater attraction both exciting and beautiful.

Diving here suits both beginners and advanced divers because the wreck rests in shallow water. As you explore, you’ll see schools of fish, sponges, and even occasional reef sharks. The easy shore entry means you can spend more time underwater enjoying the site.

Taste Balinese Cuisine

You will find rich, spicy flavors and unique local dishes when you eat in Bali. The food often features fresh herbs and traditional cooking methods that offer an authentic taste of the island. Two important ways to enjoy Balinese food are eating at a local warung and trying the famous Babi Guling.

Dine at a Local Warung

Warungs are small, family-run eateries found throughout Bali. Eating at one lets you try home-cooked meals made from fresh, local ingredients. Warungs usually serve dishes like nasi campur (mixed rice with various sides) or mie goreng (fried noodles).

The atmosphere is casual and budget-friendly, perfect for a quick meal or a relaxed lunch. You can expect simple seating and friendly service. Don't be surprised if the menu changes daily, based on what’s available fresh that day.

Eating at a warung gives you a chance to taste real Balinese flavors while supporting local families.

Try Babi Guling

Babi Guling is a special roasted pork dish from Bali, often served at ceremonies and celebrations. It's marinated with spices like turmeric, garlic, and coriander before being roasted over an open fire.

When you order Babi Guling, you'll get tender, flavorful meat with crispy skin. It usually comes with rice and spicy sambal (chili sauce). This dish is a must-try if you want to experience one of Bali's most famous culinary traditions.

Look for places known for Babi Guling, often in towns like Ubud or near temples, to enjoy the best versions made by expert cooks.

Day Trips from Bali

You can easily leave the busy island of Bali to visit nearby places with stunning views and unique experiences. These trips offer a chance to explore different landscapes, beaches, and local culture within a day.

Visit Nusa Penida

Nusa Penida is a quiet island just off Bali’s southeast coast. You’ll reach it by a short fast boat ride from Sanur or other ports. The island is famous for its steep cliffs, clear waters, and hidden beaches.

Key spots include Kelingking Beach, which looks like a T-Rex shape from above, and Angel’s Billabong, a natural rock pool. Be ready for some hiking and uneven paths, but the views reward your effort.

Nusa Penida is also good for snorkeling around coral reefs with turtles and colorful fish. Plan your trip early as boats can fill up fast, especially during peak seasons.

Explore Gili Islands

The Gili Islands are three small islands near Lombok, accessible by a longer boat ride from Bali’s west coast. You can choose Gili Trawangan, Gili Air, or Gili Meno, each offering calm waters and coral reefs.

These islands have no cars, so you explore by bike or on foot. You’ll find white sandy beaches and good spots for diving and snorkeling. Sea turtles are common in the clear, shallow water.

Gili Trawangan is the most lively, with more restaurants and nightlife. Gili Air and Meno are quieter, good if you want a peaceful escape. Boats run regularly, but book tickets ahead to secure a ride.
